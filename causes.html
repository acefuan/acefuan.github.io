The Greenhouse Effect

Discovered by Eunice Foote in 1856, is the increase in temperature of the earth's surface caused by the accumulation of greenhouse gases in the planet's atmosphere. These greenhouse gases include:


Carbon dioxide enters the atmosphere through activities such as burning of fossil fuels, solid waste, trees, and also a result of certain chemical reactions. Natural processes such as volcanic eruptions and decomposition also releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

A by-product of the production of fossil fuels, agricultural practices, and decay of organic waste in municipal solid waste landfills. Analysis indicates that a large contribution to the increase of methane comes from biogenic sources, such as wetlands or rice paddies.

Flourinated gases are synthetic chemical compounds that are emitted from a variety of household, commercial, and industrial applications and processes. Fluorinated gases include Hydrofluorocarbons (HFCs), Perfluorocarbons (PFCs), Sulfur Hexafluoride (SF6), and Hitrogen Trifluoride (NF3).


What are Greenhouse Gases?

Greenhouse gases are gases that trap heat in the atmosphere. According to NASA, the atmosphere is made up of approximately 78.09% nitrogen, 20.95% oxygen and 0.93% argon. That makes the percentage of greenhouse gases at a smithereen of 0.03% of the entire atmosphere. These gases that only make up for such a small percentage of the atmosphere have a tendency of absorbing radiating heat from the sun and preventing the heat from escaping the earth's atmosphere.

Although but a tiny fraction of the atmosphere, greenhouse gases are increasing day by day. In fact, these heat radiating gases has increased by 50% since the 1990 causing a global climate change. The effects bringing catastrophic incidents such as hurricanes, droughts, heat waves, wildfires, the shrinkage of the arctic sea ice and many more.

Human Activities

Although natural occurrences such as volanic eruption can contribute to the increase of greenhouse gases, the main contributor for such a rise in greenhouse gases and climate changes are due to human activities. Human activities have become much more advanced over the pass decades, which has increased the demand of certain resources such as fossil fuel, energy, etc. Which in the process of obtaining these resources causing consequences of climate changes. In spite of the fact that we are aware of the complication of climate change and are already working on solutions to adapt and solve climate issues, the issue is far from being solved and the following human activites must be addressed.


Transportation

The transportation sector is one of the largest generator for greenhouse gas emisions. Greenhouse gases such as carbon dioxide (CO2) are emitted from fuel combustion from transportation vehicles like cars, trucks, ships and airplanes. These fuel are petroleum-based products such as gasoline and diesel fuel that also emits little amounts of methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) during combustion.

Energy production

Despite the promotion and high usage of natural energy such as electric energy, an estimation of 60% of electricity actually comes from burning fossil fuels like coals and natural gases. Large amounts of carbon dioxide (CO2) alongside with small amount of methane (CH4) and nitrous oxide (N2O) are released to produce electricity through the burning of coals in a boiler that produces steam that powers a generator that creates electricity.

Industry

The production of goods and raw material for human beings usually involves in factories that conduct heavy energy usage that usually from burning fossil fuels. Some industrial factories also emit greenhouse gases during chemical reactions as a result of producing goods.

Agricultural

Agricultural activities such as crop and livestock production for food can also contribute to the emission of greenhouse gases. In commercial, nitrogen is essential for crop growing and mass production. The high levels of nitrogen containing in the soil when interacted with exessive water can cause a chemical reaction of producing nitrous oxide (N2O) into the atmosphere. Bacterials that grow in flooded rice paddies can also emit methane (CH4) into the atmosphere.

Forestry and Land-Use

Trees and plants absorb carbon dioxide (CO2) to grow and releases other natural gases that benefits us. When commercial and commercial sectors became essential to us, deforestation happened for the purpose of residences and other commercial activities. Deforestation not only removes natural carbon dioxide (CO2) extinguishers, carbon stored in these plants are also emitted into the atmosphere.
